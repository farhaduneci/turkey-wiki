# Finding and Registering at a University in Istanbul

Notes on the practical side of picking a university and getting registered — as opposed to the [list of universities](universities-in-istanbul.md) itself.

## The Tiers

From what I've seen, universities in Istanbul roughly split into three tiers:

1. **Public universities** — generally the most respected, but the application process for foreign students is more complex and tuition/procedures are less straightforward to navigate than at private universities.
2. **Private universities, upper tier** — legitimate institutions with real academic programs, comparable in spirit to the public ones but usually with a simpler admissions process for foreigners.
3. **Private universities, lower tier(s)** — in practice, some private universities exist mainly to sell enrollment as a path to a residence permit rather than an education. Students at this tier often have no real academic background, and diplomas/transcripts presented for admission aren't always genuine. This is a known pattern for people who want a residence permit and are using student status as the vehicle to get it, not an accusation against any specific school — do your own diligence.

> This is a sensitive generalization based on personal observation, not a verified ranking of any named institution. Treat it as a heads-up to look closely at a school (accreditation, actual campus/classes, other students) before committing, not as a blacklist.

## Approximate Costs

Rough figures, based on what I've seen — **treat these as a starting point, not current pricing**, since tuition and discounts change often and vary a lot by program and negotiation:

- Lower-tier private universities: master's programs with thesis (*tezli*) starting around **$2,000 USD**; other programs (non-thesis master's, bachelor's, etc.) roughly **$3,000–$6,000 USD**.
- Upper-tier private universities and public universities: noticeably higher tuition, and a more involved registration process.

Universities frequently offer discounts off the listed tuition — it's worth asking rather than assuming the published price is final.

## Required Documents

To register at a private university, you'll typically need:

- A residential address (rental contract) in Turkey
- Passport
- Diploma and transcript from your prior education, both **translated** (and typically notarized) — see [Document Translation: Original vs. Copy](../mistakes/document-translation.md) before you hand anything to a translator, it's an easy mistake to make and expensive to fix
- A Turkish phone number — see [SIM Cards, eSIM & Mobile Data](../internet/sim-cards-and-mobile-data.md)
- 3–4 biometric passport-style photos — see below

### Photo requirements

These aren't your home country's standard passport photo size — Turkey uses its own dimensions (roughly 5×6 cm from what I've seen, but confirm the exact spec yourself before getting them printed). They also need to be biometric: ears visible and clear, lips clear, eyes clear. Getting a compliant photo accepted on the residence permit application website can be more finicky than it sounds — budget extra time for this step if your first attempt gets rejected.

## Finding a University

Using the [list of universities](universities-in-istanbul.md), a reasonable approach:

1. Go through the list site by site — check the "International Students" or "International Office" section of each university's website (sometimes under a "Higher Education" heading).
2. Note program offerings, campus location, and tuition for each one that interests you.
3. Do a handful per day rather than all at once — this takes time, and you'll want to compare options rather than rush a decision.
4. Don't fully trust the numbers on the website — published tuition and program info isn't always current.

As with a lot of things in Turkey, a lot of the real, up-to-date information only comes from showing up in person — expect to visit universities directly rather than relying solely on their websites, even though it's an extra hassle.

## Registration and Residence Permit Procedure

Once you've picked a university, the rough sequence is:

1. Apply to the university.
2. Receive an offer/acceptance letter.
3. Complete in-person registration on the date the university gives you.
4. Submit your documents to the university; the university then starts your residence permit application with the Turkish government on your behalf.
5. Go to a Provincial Directorate of Migration Management (foreigners/göç idaresi) branch to register your fingerprints.
6. The university proceeds with the residence permit application using your fingerprint registration.
7. Wait for the residence permit card to arrive.

Once your application is filed, the university passes you a document from the foreigners office confirming you're in the waiting period. See [The 90-Day Legal Stay Limit](../visa/90-day-legal-stay.md#the-application-document) — I believe (not fully confirmed) this document is what protects you if you're stopped by police after your original 90 days but while your residence permit is still pending.
