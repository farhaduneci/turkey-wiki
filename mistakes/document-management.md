# Keeping Copies of Your Documents

## The Mistake

Living in Turkey means accumulating a steady stream of official paperwork — residence permit, tax documents, notarized translations, rental contracts, bank paperwork, and more. Early on I kept these scattered across email attachments, random phone folders, and physical folders, which made it hard to find anything when I needed it — and made it harder to double-check details like [dates and numbers](date-number-formats.md) against older documents.

## What I Do Now

- **Photograph every official document as soon as you get it.** Keep a dedicated album in your phone's gallery just for this (separate from regular photos), so it's easy to find and you always have copies with you even without signal or internet.
- **Run a self-hosted document management system** to keep scans organized, searchable, and tagged rather than dumped in a folder. [Paperless-ngx](https://docs.paperless-ngx.com/) is a solid open-source option — it OCRs documents on import, lets you tag and search full text, and you control where the data lives.

Having a searchable digital copy of everything also makes it much easier to catch mistakes like swapped dates or numbers — you can quickly pull up the original and compare, instead of relying on memory.
