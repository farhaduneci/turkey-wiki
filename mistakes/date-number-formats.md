# Date and Number Formats in Turkey

## The Mistake

I'm used to writing dates as `DD/MM/YYYY` or `MM/DD/YYYY`. In Turkey, the standard format is **`DD.MM.YYYY`** — note both differences: the **dot** as separator (not a slash), and the **day always comes first**.

I filled out an official document without paying attention to this, and it ended up with my day and month swapped. Nobody catches this at a glance — the numbers still "look like a date," they're just the wrong date.

**Lesson: always check every field on an official document yourself before accepting it, even ones you're sure must be correct.** A document being handed to you by an official, a bank, or a notary is not a guarantee it's correct — mistakes (yours or theirs) happen, and fixing them after the fact costs far more time than checking upfront.

## Format Reference

| | Turkey | Common alternatives elsewhere |
|---|---|---|
| Date | `DD.MM.YYYY` (e.g. `17.07.2026`), dot-separated, day first | `DD/MM/YYYY`, `MM/DD/YYYY`, `YYYY-MM-DD` |
| Decimal separator | comma `,` (e.g. `1,5` for one and a half) | period `.` |
| Thousands separator | period `.` (e.g. `1.000.000` for one million) | comma `,` |

The number formatting matters just as much as the date format — `1.234,56` in Turkey means one thousand two hundred thirty-four point five six, which is easy to misread if you're used to `1,234.56`.
