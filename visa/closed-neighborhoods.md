# Closed Neighborhoods (Kapalı Mahalle) — and the June 2026 Reopening

> ⚠️ This has changed direction more than once and is exactly the kind of rule that can change again with little notice. Verify current status on [e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr](https://e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr/) or with your local migration office (PDMM) before signing a lease or filing an application based on this page.

## Background: What "Closed" Meant

Since mid-2022, Turkey's Migration Management (Göç İdaresi) restricted new residence permit applications in specific neighborhoods (mahalle, not whole districts) where the foreign population had passed roughly **20% of local residents**. At peak, well over 1,000 neighborhoods across dozens of provinces were on this list, including well-known Istanbul areas like Esenyurt, Fatih, Küçükçekmece, Başakşehir, Bağcılar, Avcılar, Bahçelievler, Sultangazi, Esenler, and Zeytinburnu.

If a neighborhood was closed, registering an address there for a **new** residence permit application would get rejected — this applied at the neighborhood level, not the district as a whole, so two addresses a few streets apart could have different outcomes.

## The June 2026 Reopening

**As of June 10, 2026, the closed-neighborhood restriction was lifted across Istanbul** — previously-closed neighborhoods, including the ones listed above, reopened to new residence permit applications, and Istanbul is no longer operating under a closed-district system.

Some reporting immediately after the change described it inconsistently — a few sources described Fatih and Esenyurt as still excluded even as others described the reopening as covering all of Istanbul. Treat "closed neighborhoods no longer apply in Istanbul" as the current, most-supported picture, but don't take it as a permanent guarantee — the list (and the policy itself) has been added to, trimmed, and reversed before.

## How to Verify Before You Rely on This

- Check your specific address directly in the [e-ikamet](https://e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr/) system when you start your application — it will tell you at the point of address registration if a neighborhood is rejected.
- If in doubt, call **157** (YİMER, the Foreigners Communication Center) — see [Essential Daily Phrases](../guides/essential-daily-phrases.md).
- Don't sign a lease purely on the assumption a neighborhood is open — confirm first, especially if you're outside Istanbul, since this page only covers the Istanbul-wide reopening.
