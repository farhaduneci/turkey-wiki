# The 90-Day Legal Stay Limit

> ⚠️ This is one of the most important things to understand about staying in Turkey. Getting it wrong has severe, immediate consequences — not just a fine.

## The Rule

Your legal stay in Turkey starts on your **entrance date** — the date stamped in your passport at the border/airport. For most nationalities under visa-exemption or e-visa arrangements, this gives you **90 days** of legal stay (rules vary by nationality and visa type, so confirm your specific allowance — this may change, so double check current rules before relying on it).

You must either:

- Leave the country before the 90 days are up, or
- Complete your **residence permit (ikamet)** application and have it approved/in process before the 90 days expire.

## Why This Matters So Much

If you overstay without a valid residence permit in progress, you are not just facing a fine or a warning:

- Police can stop you at any time and ask for your passport or ID.
- If you're found to have overstayed, you can be **detained on the spot, in the street, wherever you are** — with no advance warning and no chance to first go home and get your belongings.
- You can be taken to an **immigration removal center** and **deported**, potentially with a re-entry ban.

There is no grace period you can count on. Treat the 90-day mark as a hard deadline.

## The Application Document

Once your residence permit application is actually submitted (fingerprints done, application filed — e.g. via a university in a student case, see [Finding and Registering at a University in Istanbul](../student-life/finding-and-registering-university.md)), you should get a document back from the foreigners office (through whoever filed on your behalf) confirming you're in the waiting period.

I believe — but haven't fully confirmed — that this document is what covers you past the 90-day mark: if the police stop you after your 90 days are technically up but your application is filed and pending, this is what you show them to prove you're legally in process rather than overstaying. **Don't rely on my word alone for this** — verify with 157 (YİMER) or a lawyer, and carry the document once you have it either way.

## Practical Precautions

- **Always carry your passport (or residence permit card, once issued) with you.** Police checks can happen anywhere, at any time.
- **Photograph the passport page with your photo and the page with your entrance stamp** as soon as you enter the country, and keep those photos accessible on your phone. See [Keeping Copies of Your Documents](../mistakes/document-management.md) for a more general approach to storing document photos and copies.
- **Start your residence permit application well before the deadline**, not at the last minute — appointment slots can be booked out for weeks.
- Double-check your entrance date and count the 90 days carefully. Given how [dates are written in Turkey](../mistakes/date-number-formats.md) (`DD.MM.YYYY`), make sure you're reading the stamp date correctly and not swapping day and month.
- If you have questions about your residence permit or visa status, call **157**, the 24/7 YİMER (Foreigners Communication Center) hotline — see [Essential Daily Phrases](../guides/essential-daily-phrases.md) for details.
